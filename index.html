My Experiences


As mentioned before, I'm currently studying Computer Science and Engineering at UPRM. I've worked on projects within my courses, along with being part of the IEEE Computer Society Web Development Team. As far as projects go, I've worked most of them at my Data Structures course, where we were given three videogame projects along the semester. We first were assigned a parody version of the arcade game Frogger, in which we were assigned to fulfill specs missing from the game, such as adding missing obstacles, implementing the map boundaries, correctly adding score and making sure a "Game Over" was obtainable. For the second project, an original videogame called Project: Envy was assigned, and RPG inspired game. For this one we also had to add different missing aspects, such as a quest system, an Non-Playable Character (NPC) with conditional dialogue, and obstacles to prevent the player from getting to the next area unless the quest was completed. As for the last project, a parody version of the Mario Maker videogame, where we were assigned to enable multiplayer in multiple screens, both characters with their individual focused camera, as well as adding new building blocks for the integrated map building interface.
